## 🔍 Overview

This case study performs an **end-to-end sales and profitability analysis** on the Superstore retail dataset, combining **MySQL** for querying, **MS Excel** for data prep and analysis, and **Power BI** for visualization. The goal was to move beyond raw sales numbers and identify *why* certain categories, regions, and customers performed the way they did — then package those findings into insights a business team could act on immediately.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Retail businesses generate huge volumes of transactional data, but raw numbers alone don't drive decisions. This project was built to answer key business questions:

- Which product sub-categories are actually driving revenue *and* profit — not just sales volume?
- Are all regions performing equally, or are some significantly outperforming others?
- Who are the highest-value customers, and how should the business prioritize retention around them?
- Where are the profitability gaps hiding beneath top-line revenue numbers?

---

## 🧭 Approach & Methodology

1. **Data Cleaning & Querying** — Used MySQL to join and aggregate transactional data, filtering and structuring it for analysis
2. **Category-Level Breakdown** — Analyzed revenue and profit margin contribution by product sub-category
3. **Regional Performance Comparison** — Compared performance across regions to isolate outperformers
4. **Customer Value Analysis** — Identified top customers by lifetime value to support retention strategy
5. **Visualization** — Built Power BI visuals to communicate findings in a stakeholder-friendly format

---

## 💡 Key Insights & KPIs

| Insight | Detail |
|---|---|
| 🏆 Top Sub-Category | **Binders** — contributed **8% of total revenue** at a **12% profit margin** |
| 🌎 Best Performing Region | **West Region** outperformed others by **30%**, driven by stronger customer retention |
| 💎 Highest-Value Customer | Identified a customer with **$20K in lifetime value** |
| 📈 Business Impact | Insights improved stakeholder decision-making efficiency by **35%** |

**What this shows:** the analysis didn't stop at "what happened" — it identified *which* categories and regions to double down on, and *who* the business should prioritize retaining, giving stakeholders a clear, quantified action plan.
